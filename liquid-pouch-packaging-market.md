<p><p>The liquid pouch packaging market comprises two primary types: rigid and flexible packaging. Rigid packaging typically involves hard materials, providing structural integrity and protection for liquids, ideal for products requiring stability, such as sauces or dairy. In contrast, flexible packaging uses pliable materials that conform to the shape of the contents, offering lightweight, space-efficient solutions that minimize waste. Flexible pouches are increasingly favored for beverages and snacks due to their convenience, resealability, and cost-effectiveness, catering to diverse consumer needs.</p></p>
